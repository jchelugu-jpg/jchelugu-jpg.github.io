MENTAL HEALTH AWARNESS

    EMOTIONAL

Emotional Well-Being involves primarly the ability to understand, manage, and express emotions in a healthy manner. Often it involves having the resilience to cope with stress, the capacity to experience joy, and the skill to process complex emotions like anger or grief without becoming overwhelmed.

    PHYSCHOLOGICAL

Physchological Well-being focuses on your internal mental state and how you preceive yourself and your life's direction. This involves having a sense of self-acceptance, a feeling of personal growth, and a clear sense of purpose. In essence, it's less about how you "feel" in the moment and rahter more about your cognitive evaluatioin to your life; a common example is believing that your life has meaning and that you have the autonomy to make your own choices.

    SOCIAL

Social well-being involves the quaility of your relationships and how you intergrate into your community. It reveolves around your ability to form meaningful connections, offer and recieve support, and maintain healthy boundaries. It's human nature to be social, so feeling a sense of belonging and having a reliable support system are critical components that protect against isolation and improve overall mental helath.
