MENTAL HEALTH AWARNESS

It takes a lot of strength and courage to realize you're depressed and could use some help. Like everygone else, you deserve compassion and care when you're not felling well.

Even when part of you knows you need help, it can feel really overwhelming or stressful, but don't let it stop you from openining up. Talking about mental health can be hard and awkaward at fist, but it's worth pushing through so you can start to feel better.

Here are some things that can make talking about mental health easier.

    Ask yourself these questions

  • How have I been feeling and how does it impact my day?
  • What has changed in my mood, sleep, relationships, and life at school or work?
  • What feelings or thoughts keep runnning through my mind (positive or negative)?
  • How long have I been noticing these thoughts or feelings?

      • Make a Plan

  • Decide who, family, friend, therapist, etc..in your life is best equipped to provide the help you need in that moment.
  • Establish a clear understanding of what help you're seeking and what you need today. Do you need help with finding professional support? Love and comfort? Or just someone to listen? Tell them this up front to reduce any confusion.
  • Identify an appropriate place ad time to have a private conversation. If the person is in another location ask them to find a space that is private too for their full attention.

      • How to talk about it

  • It may be difficult to summarize your needs. Start slowly and communicate inital needs clearly to avoid confusion or assumptions.
  • When you talk, describe your feelings, thoughts, moods, and how your body feels. Give them a clear picture of your situation - tell them what you need.
  • If you talk to a health care professional, they will ask you questions to adhere to you best. They will ask you questions to understand your needs. Answering clearly and honestly helps them help you.
